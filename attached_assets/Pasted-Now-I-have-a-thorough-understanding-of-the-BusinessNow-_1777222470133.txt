Improved Prompt: Timesheet Module — AI Assistant & Guardrails

🧩 Context
This is the Timesheet Module of BusinessNow PSA — the most critical module used by every employee across all projects. It must be accurate, guided, and enforceable. The AI assistant embedded in this module must serve as both a smart assistant and a compliance enforcer.

📋 Part 1: Timesheet Entry Display Rules
When an employee opens their timesheet, the system must:

Show only their assigned work — display each row as: Project Name → Phase → Task → Subtask (respecting the full task hierarchy), pre-filtered to only the projects and tasks where the employee has an active allocation for that week.
Pre-populate from allocations — auto-fill the timesheet grid with the employee's allocated projects/tasks using their hoursPerWeek from the allocations table, so they only need to confirm or adjust, not start from scratch.
Show allocation context inline — next to each project row, display: Allocated Hours/Week, Total Allocated Hours (project duration), Hours Logged So Far, and Remaining Hours. This gives the employee full visibility without leaving the timesheet.
Enforce timecode/activity selection — each time entry must require the employee to select the correct Timecode Category → Activity → Billable/Non-Billable flag, aligned to the firm's taxonomy.


🚨 Part 2: Guardrails & Validation Rules
The following rules must be enforced at the point of entry, not just at submission:
Rule 1 — Daily Hour Cap
Block or warn if a single day's total logged hours (across all projects) exceeds the employee's configured daily capacity (default: 8 hours). Alert: "You have logged X hours on [date], which exceeds your daily capacity of Y hours. Please review before saving."
Rule 2 — Weekly Allocation Overrun
If the hours logged for a project in a given week exceed the employee's hoursPerWeek allocation for that project, raise a soft warning: "You have logged X hours on [Project Name] this week, which exceeds your weekly allocation of Y hours. Billed hours are more than planned — please recheck before submitting."
Rule 3 — Project Budget Overrun (Cumulative)
If cumulative hours logged by the employee on a project are approaching or exceed their totalHours allocation (e.g., ≥90% threshold = warning, ≥100% = hard block with override requiring PM approval), alert: "Your total logged hours on [Project Name] have reached X of your Y allocated hours. Contact your Project Manager before logging additional hours."
Rule 4 — Duplicate Entry Detection
If the employee attempts to log time for the same Project + Task + Date combination more than once, warn: "A time entry already exists for [Task] on [Date]. Do you want to add to it or replace it?"
Rule 5 — Weekend / Non-Working Day Guard
Block time entries on weekends or configured public holidays (using the employee's assigned Holiday Calendar), unless explicitly enabled by admin in time_settings. Alert: "[Date] is a non-working day. Are you sure you want to log time here?"
Rule 6 — Unsubmitted Timesheet Reminder
If the current week's timesheet is in Draft status past the configured timesheetDueDay and timesheetDueTime, automatically surface a reminder banner: "Your timesheet for the week of [date] is due. Please review and submit by [deadline]."
Rule 7 — Minimum Hours Submission Gate
Enforce the minSubmitHours setting — prevent submission if total hours are below the configured minimum. Alert: "You have only logged X hours this week. The minimum required for submission is Y hours."
Rule 8 — Maximum Hours Submission Gate
Enforce the maxSubmitHours setting — prevent submission if total hours exceed the configured maximum. Alert: "Your total logged hours of X exceed the maximum allowed of Y hours per week. Please review."
Rule 9 — Time Entry on Inactive / Ended Project
Block time entries on projects where the employee's allocation endDate has passed. Alert: "Your allocation on [Project Name] ended on [date]. You cannot log time to this project. Contact your PM to extend your allocation if needed."
Rule 10 — Billable Hours Ratio Anomaly
If an employee's billable percentage for the week falls significantly below their historical average (e.g., >20% drop) or their project's expected billable ratio, surface a soft alert for the approver: "[Employee]'s billable hours this week are X%, which is significantly below their average of Y%. Review before approving."
Rule 11 — Self-Approval Prevention
Enforce the existing rule: an employee cannot approve their own timesheet, even if they hold an approver role on the project.
Rule 12 — Notes Required on Rejection
When an approver rejects a timesheet, a rejection note must be mandatory, not optional. The note is surfaced to the employee in the notification and inline in the timesheet Draft view.

🤖 Part 3: AI Assistant for Timesheet Filling
The AI assistant must act as a smart co-pilot embedded within the timesheet grid. It should:
Auto-Suggest Time Entries
Based on the employee's active allocations, recent timesheet patterns, and open tasks assigned to them, the AI proactively suggests: "Based on your allocations this week, here is a suggested timesheet. Review and confirm, or edit as needed." The suggestion pre-fills Project, Task, Hours (from hoursPerWeek / workingDaysInWeek), and Billable flag.
Natural Language Entry
Allow the employee to type in plain language, e.g., "I worked 3 hours on Oracle WMS data mapping today" — and the AI maps this to the correct Project → Task → Timecode, prompts for confirmation, and logs the entry.
Guardrail Enforcement in Conversation
The AI must apply all guardrails in Part 2 conversationally. If a user types "log 10 hours on HarbourLink EDI today", the AI must respond: "That would exceed your daily capacity of 8 hours and your weekly allocation of 32 hours for this project. Would you like me to split this across two days instead, or adjust the hours?"
Allocation Awareness
Before logging any entry, the AI must check the employee's hoursPerWeek, totalHours, and cumulative hours logged and surface this context: "You have 12 hours remaining on your HarbourLink allocation. This entry would use 4 of them."
Missing Timesheet Detection
At the start of each working day, the AI proactively checks for unlogged days in the current week and prompts: "You haven't logged time for [Monday, Tuesday]. Would you like me to suggest entries based on your schedule?"
Explanation of Alerts
When a guardrail triggers, the AI must explain clearly why: "I'm flagging this because your planned allocation for [Project] is 24 hours/week, and you've already logged 22 hours this week. Adding 4 more would put you 2 hours over plan. Should I notify your PM?"
Escalation to PM
If an employee's hours are consistently over or under allocation across multiple weeks, the AI should generate a summary alert for the Project Manager or approver: "[Employee] has exceeded their weekly allocation on [Project] for 3 consecutive weeks. Review may be needed."
Audit Trail Awareness
The AI must never silently overwrite an existing approved or locked entry. If a user asks to edit a locked entry, the AI responds: "This entry is locked after approval. Please contact your approver or an admin to unlock it."

✅ Part 4: Submission & Approval Flow

Employee submits → system validates all guardrails → if clean, status moves to Submitted and the resolved approver is notified.
Approver sees a summary: total hours, billable %, allocation compliance status (green / amber / red per project), and any flagged entries.
Approver can approve, reject (with mandatory note), or request clarification via the in-timesheet message thread.
On rejection, the timesheet returns to Draft with the rejection note visible inline, and the employee is notified.
Bulk approval is supported for PMs managing large teams, with automated flagging of any timesheets with unresolved guardrail warnings.


📊 Part 5: Reporting Hooks
Every guardrail event (warning triggered, override requested, anomaly flagged) must be logged for downstream reporting. The reports module should expose: Allocation vs. Actuals by resource and project, Billable utilisation %, Over/under allocation trends, and Timesheet submission compliance rates (submitted on time vs. late).
